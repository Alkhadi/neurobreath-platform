Depression involves persistent low mood or loss of interest for weeks or longer, often paired with disrupted sleep, appetite, and energy. It affects motivation, concentration, and confidence in everyday routines.

Common signs

What it is

A mood condition with persistent low mood and/or loss of interest, alongside changes in energy, sleep or appetite.

Possible signs

  • Persistent sadness or loss of pleasure
  • Tiredness; low energy; slowed thinking
  • Poor concentration or indecisiveness
  • Sleep or appetite changes; feelings of worthlessness

Evidence & support snapshot ¬∑ UK ‚Üî US

Use these stats and contacts during safety planning, GP reviews or workplace adjustments.

Prevalence & impact

  • United Kingdom: about 1 in 5 adults report symptoms of anxiety or depression in any given week; major depressive episodes affect roughly 3‚Äì4% of adults annually and are rising fastest in young people (NHS Digital 2023).
  • United States: 15% of adults experience a major depressive episode each year and 23% live with a mental illness overall (NAMI 2024). Rates are higher among 18‚Äì25 year olds.
  • Sleep & stress: 30% of adults in both countries report insomnia symptoms; sleep disruption amplifies low mood, so pair breathing with sleep hygiene.
  • Breathing & movement: breathwork meta-analyses (2023) and exercise trials show small-to-moderate improvements in mood when used consistently alongside therapy or medication.

Support & crisis lines

  • UK: NHS Talking Therapies (self-referral for CBT, counselling), Mind info line 0300 123 3393, Samaritans 116 123 (24/7), CALM 0800 58 58 58 (5pm‚Äìmidnight), SHOUT text 85258, Papyrus HOPELINEUK 0800 068 4141 for under 35s.
  • US: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call/text), Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741), NAMI HelpLine 1-800-950-NAMI, SAMHSA treatment locator 1-800-662-HELP, Trevor Project (LGBTQ+ youth) 1-866-488-7386.
  • Self-guided supports: behavioural activation (tiny actions), slow coherent breathing (5 in/5 out) to reduce rumination, morning light exposure, gentle movement and peer groups (Mind, NAMI, local charities).
  • Medical care: SSRIs/SNRIs, talking therapies (CBT, IPT, ACT) and combined care remain the most evidence-backed treatments ‚Äî contact your GP/PCP for assessment.
