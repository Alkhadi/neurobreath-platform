Practical tools and guidance for using NeuroBreath in classrooms, groups, and one-to-one sessions.

Using NeuroBreath in Group Settings

NeuroBreath tools are designed to work both individually and in group settings. Here's how to facilitate group breathing and focus sessions:

Group Breathing Sessions

  1. Prepare the space: Ensure a calm, quiet environment. Dim lights if possible. Ask students to put devices on silent.
  2. Choose an activity: Start with a simple breathing exercise like 60-second SOS or Box Breathing.
  3. Individual devices: Each student can use their own device to follow along, or you can project one screen for the whole class.
  4. Set expectations: Explain that this is practice, not a test. Everyone can participate at their own pace.
  5. Start together: Count down "3, 2, 1, begin" and let students start their timers.
  6. Monitor engagement: Walk around gently. Students who are following along will show calm body language.
  7. Celebrate participation: After the session, acknowledge everyone's effort. No need to track who "did it right."

Using Progress Tracking in Groups

The Progress Dashboard and Rewards System can be used in group settings:

  • Individual progress: Each student tracks their own progress on their device (stored locally, no accounts needed).
  • Class challenges: Use the "30-Day Calm Challenge" to encourage regular practice. See the Blog for challenge details.
  • Badges and rewards: Students can earn badges individually. Consider creating a class display board for celebrating achievements.
  • Printable certificates: Students can print their certificates from the Rewards page to take home or display.

Focus Games for Groups

The Focus Training Games work well for:

  • Transition activities: Use a 2-minute focus game between subjects to help students reset.
  • Individual practice: Students can play focus games during independent work time.
  • Calm corners: Set up a device with focus games in a quiet corner for students who need a break.

Breathing Games for Groups

The Breathing Games (Calm Bubble, Dragon Calm, Balloon Float) are perfect for:

  • Whole-class calming: Project one game on the board and have everyone breathe together.
  • Small group practice: Students can work in pairs or small groups, taking turns.
  • Individual support: Use games one-to-one with students who need extra support.

Low-Stimulation Mode

All games and exercises include a "Low Stimulation Mode" toggle. This is especially useful for:

  • Students with sensory sensitivities
  • Reducing distractions in busy classrooms
  • Creating a calmer environment

Tips for Success

  • Start small: Begin with 1-2 minute sessions and gradually increase.
  • Be flexible: Some students may need to move or fidget. That's okay.
  • No pressure: Emphasize that participation is voluntary and there's no "right way" to do it.
  • Regular practice: Consistency is more important than duration. A few minutes daily is better than one long session weekly.
  • Model it: Participate yourself! Students learn by seeing adults use these tools too.
