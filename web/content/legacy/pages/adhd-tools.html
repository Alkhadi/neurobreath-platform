What it is

A neurodevelopmental condition that affects attention regulation, impulsivity and hyperactivity. Experiences vary from person to person.

Possible signs

  • Difficulty sustaining attention or following through on tasks
  • Restlessness or fidgeting; feeling ‚Äúon the go‚Äù
  • Impulsivity (e.g., interrupting, acting before thinking)

Breathing orb ‚Äî nervous system reset

Short, paced breathing helps regulate the autonomic nervous system. Use this 4-2-6 pattern before starting a focus sprint, transitioning between tasks, or when emotions run high.

  1. Inhale through the nose for a slow count of 4 as the orb expands.
  2. Hold gently for 2.
  3. Exhale through the mouth for a slow count of 6, letting shoulders drop.
  4. Repeat for 4‚Äì6 cycles (about 2‚Äì3 minutes).

Tip: For younger learners, imagine blowing bubbles. For older learners, pair the exhale with ‚ÄúIn with calm, out with tension.‚Äù

    ADHD pain points & support for young people (UK)

    Key challenges raised by UK families and schools, alongside evidence-backed support that helps students remain engaged and regulated.

    Frequent pain points

    • Persistent restless energy and reduced classroom focus that disrupt ongoing learning progress.
    • Executive-organisation difficulties leading to missed homework submissions and uneven exam preparation.
    • Emotional dysregulation with dips in self-esteem triggered by repeated corrections or disciplinary feedback.
    • Stressful transitions between school, home and online study environments that overwhelm daily routines.

    Evidence-backed responses

    • Parent/carer psychoeducation programmes ‚Äì first-line provision for children under five and a recommended offer for school-age learners (NICE NG87).
    • Behavioural and organisational coaching ‚Äì structured reward systems, visual schedules and planner check-ins to scaffold executive skills (NHS ADHD treatment guide).
    • Talking therapies (CBT/skills training) ‚Äì targeted work on problem solving and emotional regulation for older children and adolescents (YoungMinds ADHD overview).
    • Medication for severe or persistent symptoms ‚Äì methylphenidate, lisdexamfetamine or atomoxetine prescribed within shared-care agreements following informed consent (NICE NG87).
    • Movement and active breaks ‚Äì short bursts of vigorous play or structured sport to lift mood, attention and body regulation (Youth Sport Trust ‚Äì Active in Mind).
