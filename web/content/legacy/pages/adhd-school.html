Reasonable adjustments that often help

In class

  • Visual schedules and now/next boards to make transitions predictable.
  • Clear instructions one or two steps at a time with check-back.
  • Movement options: stand-up desk, elastic bands on chair legs, short movement breaks.
  • Access to fidget or sensory tools agreed in advance.

Around campus

  • Quiet reset space for processing overload or emotions without isolation.
  • Buddy or mentor system for tricky transitions (arrival, lunch, end of day).
  • Timetable or locker check-ins so equipment and homework make it home.
  • Clear, consistent behaviour policy that separates deliberate choices from ADHD difficulties.

Assessment & exams

  • Rest breaks, smaller rooms and additional time where concentration is affected.
  • Alternative ways to show learning: oral presentations, assistive tech, scaffolded writing.
  • Planning support for coursework (milestones, visual planners, check-in dates).
  • Link exam adjustments to the day-to-day support plan so it feels consistent.
